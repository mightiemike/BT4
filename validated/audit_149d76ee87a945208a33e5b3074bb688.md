### Title
Unclaimed Airdrop Tokens Are Permanently Locked in `Airdrop.sol` — No Recovery Mechanism Exists - (File: `core/contracts/Airdrop.sol`)

---

### Summary

`Airdrop.sol` can receive ERC20 tokens and distribute them via Merkle-proof claims, but contains no function allowing the owner or any party to recover unclaimed tokens. Any tokens deposited into the contract that are not claimed by users are permanently locked with no retrieval path.

---

### Finding Description

The `Airdrop` contract holds a `token` address set at initialization and transfers tokens to claimants via `SafeERC20.safeTransfer` inside `_claim()`. The owner can register weekly Merkle roots via `registerMerkleRoot()`, and users call `claim()` to receive their allocations. [1](#0-0) 

However, the contract exposes **no function** to:
- Withdraw tokens that were never claimed after a campaign ends
- Recover tokens accidentally sent to the contract
- Sweep residual balances after all valid claim windows have passed [2](#0-1) 

The full public/external interface of `Airdrop.sol` consists of only: `initialize`, `registerMerkleRoot`, `claim`, and `getClaimed`. None of these allow outbound token movement except to the claiming user. [3](#0-2) 

---

### Impact Explanation

Any ERC20 tokens deposited into `Airdrop.sol` that are not claimed — due to users not participating, incorrect Merkle roots being registered, campaigns ending, or simply excess funding — are **permanently irrecoverable**. The `token` balance of the contract can only decrease via `_claim()`, which requires a valid Merkle proof from a non-sanctioned, non-duplicate claimant. There is no other outbound transfer path.

This is a direct loss-of-funds scenario: protocol-owned tokens sent to fund the airdrop contract are locked forever if not fully distributed. [4](#0-3) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

In practice, airdrop campaigns routinely have unclaimed balances. Not all eligible users claim within the active window, some addresses may be sanctioned (blocking their claim), and over-funded contracts are common. The likelihood that **some** tokens remain unclaimed is high. The likelihood that the owner will eventually want to recover them is also high, and the complete absence of any recovery path makes this a realistic, concrete loss. [5](#0-4) 

---

### Recommendation

Add an owner-restricted token recovery function to `Airdrop.sol`:

```solidity
function recoverToken(address to, uint256 amount) external onlyOwner {
    SafeERC20.safeTransfer(IERC20(token), to, amount);
}
```

Optionally, enforce a time-lock (e.g., only callable after a campaign has been inactive for N weeks) to prevent premature sweeping of live campaigns.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Owner deploys and initializes `Airdrop.sol` with `token = NADO_TOKEN`.
2. Owner funds the contract by transferring `1,000,000 NADO` to `address(airdrop)`.
3. Owner calls `registerMerkleRoot(1, root)` for week 1.
4. Only 600,000 NADO worth of claims are submitted by users before the campaign ends.
5. The remaining 400,000 NADO sits in the contract.
6. Owner attempts to recover the 400,000 NADO — **no function exists to do so**.
7. The 400,000 NADO is permanently locked. [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6)
