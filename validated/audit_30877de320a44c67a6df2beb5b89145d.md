### Title
`burnNlp` Post-Burn Maintenance Health Check Permanently Locks Assets for Barely-Solvent Subaccounts — (`core/contracts/Clearinghouse.sol`)

---

### Summary

`burnNlp` applies a minimum $1 burn fee before checking post-burn maintenance health. For subaccounts whose maintenance health sits in the range `[0, health_impact_of_burn_fee)`, the burn always reverts. Because the same health gate blocks `withdrawCollateral`, and the subaccount is above the liquidation threshold, the user has no on-chain exit path and their NLP collateral is permanently locked.

---

### Finding Description

`burnNlp` in `Clearinghouse.sol` computes a burn fee of `max(ONE, quoteAmount / 1000)` — a hard floor of $1 — and credits only the net quote to the caller: [1](#0-0) 

After applying the fee and updating balances, the function enforces: [2](#0-1) 

The comment in the code acknowledges the problem: *"Burning NLP can decrease health if the burn fee exceeds the health improvement from the withdrawal."* However, the code does not provide any escape hatch for this case. [3](#0-2) 

The minimum burn fee is defined as `ONE = 1e18` ($1): [4](#0-3) 

For a subaccount whose pre-burn maintenance health is in `[0, $1_health_equivalent)`:

1. **Cannot burn NLP** — the $1 fee pushes post-burn maintenance health below zero, causing the `require` to revert.
2. **Cannot withdraw collateral** — `withdrawCollateral` also gates on `getHealth(sender, INITIAL) >= 0`. [5](#0-4) 
3. **Cannot be liquidated** — pre-burn maintenance health is `>= 0`, so `isUnderMaintenance` returns false. [6](#0-5) 

The user is trapped in a solvent-but-unexitable state. Burning a smaller NLP amount does not help: the $1 floor fee applies regardless of size, and if `quoteAmount < burnFee`, `MathHelper.max(0, quoteAmount - burnFee)` returns 0, so the user loses NLP collateral while receiving nothing, worsening health further.

Additionally, if the NLP was minted within the last 4 days, `burnNlp` also requires `getNlpUnlockedBalance >= nlpAmount`: [7](#0-6) 

The unlock check depends on `getOracleTime()` advancing past `mintTime + NLP_LOCK_PERIOD`: [8](#0-7) 

`NLP_LOCK_PERIOD` is 4 days: [9](#0-8) 

`getOracleTime()` is sourced from the sequencer-controlled `IEndpoint.getTime()`: [10](#0-9) 

So within the lock window the user cannot even attempt the burn. After the lock window expires, the post-burn health check becomes the binding constraint.

---

### Impact Explanation

A user whose NLP collateral is their primary position and whose maintenance health falls into `[0, ~$1_equivalent)` has no on-chain exit. Their NLP tokens and any co-deposited collateral are permanently locked in the protocol. The user cannot burn, withdraw, or be liquidated. The only theoretical escape is depositing additional external funds to raise health above the burn-fee threshold — which is unavailable to users who have already committed all their capital.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** The scenario requires maintenance health to be in a narrow positive band, but this is realistic for:
- Users who mint NLP with most of their available quote and experience a small adverse NLP price move.
- Users who hold NLP alongside a spot borrow where interest accrual gradually erodes health toward zero.
- Any user whose health drifts into the `[0, $1)` band between sequencer submissions.

The $1 floor fee is a fixed, protocol-defined constant, making the vulnerable band deterministic and non-negligible for small accounts.

---

### Recommendation

**Short term:** In `burnNlp`, skip the post-burn maintenance health check (or replace it with a pre-burn check) when the subaccount's pre-burn maintenance health is non-negative. The intent of the check — preventing deliberate health destruction — is already served by the pre-burn state. Alternatively, waive the burn fee when the fee itself would be the sole cause of the health violation.

**Long term:** Audit all exit paths (burn, withdraw, close) for the existence of a "dead zone" where a subaccount is above the liquidation threshold but below the exit threshold. Document and test the invariant that every solvent subaccount must have at least one reachable exit path.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice deposits $1,000 USDC as quote collateral.
2. Alice mints $1,000 worth of NLP via `mintNlp`. Her NLP balance is locked for 4 days.
3. Four days pass; NLP price drops 0.05%. Alice's maintenance health is now $0.40 (positive, not liquidatable).
4. Alice submits a `BurnNlp` transaction for her full NLP position.
   - `quoteAmount = $999.50`
   - `burnFee = max($1, $0.9995) = $1`
   - `netQuote = $998.50`
   - Post-burn maintenance health = $0.40 − $1.00 = −$0.60
   - `require(getHealth(...) >= 0)` → **reverts**
5. Alice tries `withdrawCollateral` for any amount → `require(getHealth(...) >= 0)` → **reverts**.
6. No liquidator can touch Alice because her maintenance health is $0.40 ≥ 0.
7. Alice has no additional funds to deposit. Her $1,000 of NLP collateral is permanently locked.
