### Title
`minDepositRateX18` Applied to Both Multipliers Breaks Liquidity-Preservation Invariant, Creating Protocol Insolvency — (File: `core/contracts/SpotEngineState.sol`)

---

### Summary

In `SpotEngineState._updateState`, when `minDepositRateX18 != 0`, the min-deposit-rate multiplier `M` is applied to **both** `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18` and `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18`. The code's own stated invariant — that liquidity `(deposits − borrows)` must be preserved after a state update — is broken. The result is phantom liquidity: the protocol's accounting claims more net assets than the contract actually holds, making it structurally insolvent by `liquidity × (M − 1)` per update cycle.

---

### Finding Description

`_updateState` opens with an explicit accounting invariant:

> "if we don't take fees into account, the liquidity, which is (deposits − borrows) should remain the same after updating state."
> `depositRateMultiplier = utilization × (borrowRateMultiplier − 1) + 1` [1](#0-0) 

The regular-rate block correctly satisfies this invariant. `totalDepositRateX18` is derived from `utilizationRatioX18 × (borrowRateMultiplierX18 − ONE)`, ensuring that the interest paid by borrowers exactly equals the interest received by depositors plus the fee credited to `FEES_ACCOUNT`. [2](#0-1) 

However, the `minDepositRateX18` block then multiplies **both** cumulative multipliers by the same scalar `minDepositRateMultiplierX18`:

```solidity
state.cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18 = state
    .cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18
    .mul(minDepositRateMultiplierX18);   // ← borrows grow

state.cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18 = state
    .cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18
    .mul(minDepositRateMultiplierX18);   // ← deposits grow by same factor
``` [3](#0-2) 

Let `L` = liquidity after the regular-rate step, `M` = `minDepositRateMultiplierX18 > 1`.

- Claimed liquidity after min-rate step = `L × M`
- Actual token balance of the contract = `L` (unchanged; no tokens enter or leave)
- **Phantom liquidity created per call = `L × (M − 1)`**

The `INTEREST_FEE_FRACTION` constant is 20%, confirming the fee-deduction path is separate and does not compensate for this inflation. [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**Structural insolvency proportional to free liquidity.**

Concrete scenario (ignoring the regular borrow rate for clarity):

| Step | Alice balance | Bob debt | Contract tokens |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alice deposits 100, Bob borrows 80 | 100 | 80 | 20 |
| After 1 yr, `minDepositRateX18 = 10 %` → M = 1.1 | **110** | **88** | **20** |
| Bob repays 88 | 110 | 0 | 108 |
| Alice withdraws | **fails: needs 110, has 108** | — | — |

Shortfall = `20 × (1.1 − 1) = 2 tokens`. This shortfall compounds with every `updateStates` call. Over time, the gap between promised deposits and actual tokens grows monotonically. The first depositors to withdraw are made whole at the expense of later depositors — a classic insolvency cascade.

The `assertUtilization` guard (`totalDeposits >= totalBorrows`) does **not** catch this because both sides are inflated by the same factor, leaving the ratio unchanged. [5](#0-4) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

`minDepositRateX18` is a per-product `Config` field set via `addOrUpdateProduct`. The QUOTE product is initialized to `0`, but any non-quote spot product can be configured with a non-zero value by the owner as a routine protocol parameter (e.g., to guarantee a minimum yield for liquidity providers). [6](#0-5) 

Once set, the defect is triggered automatically on every `updateStates` call from the `Endpoint`, which is a normal sequencer heartbeat — no attacker action is required beyond depositing and later withdrawing. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Recommendation

The `minDepositRateX18` multiplier must **not** be applied to `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18`. The min deposit rate is a guaranteed floor for depositors; its cost must be funded explicitly — either from the protocol's insurance/treasury account or by adjusting the borrow rate model so that the borrow rate already covers the floor before the deposit rate is derived. Applying it symmetrically to both multipliers violates the liquidity-preservation invariant the protocol itself documents and inflates the deposit side without a corresponding real-asset backing.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Algebraic proof:**

After the regular-rate step, let:
- `TD` = total deposits (denormalized), `TB` = total borrows (denormalized)
- `L = TD − TB` = actual contract token balance (liquidity)
- `M = minDepositRateMultiplierX18 > 1`

After the min-rate step:
- Claimed deposits = `TD × M`
- Claimed borrows = `TB × M`
- Claimed liquidity = `(TD − TB) × M = L × M`
- Actual token balance = `L`

Even after all borrowers repay their inflated debts (`TB × M` tokens flow in), the contract holds `L + TB × M` tokens. Total depositor claims = `TD × M`. Solvency requires:

```
L + TB × M  ≥  TD × M
L           ≥  (TD − TB) × M
L           ≥  L × M
1           ≥  M
```

This is false for any `M > 1`, i.e., any non-zero `minDepositRateX18`. The protocol is unconditionally insolvent whenever this parameter is active. [3](#0-2)
