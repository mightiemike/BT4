### Title
Checkpoint Advancement Without Bonus Payment in `slashRedelegationPosition` Causes Permanent Bonus Loss — (`x/tieredrewards/keeper/slash.go`)

---

### Summary

`slashRedelegationPosition` calls `processEventsAndClaimBonus` with a pointer to `pos`. That function advances `LastEventSeq`, `LastBonusAccrual`, and `LastKnownBonded` on the `pos` object **and** decrements event reference counts in the store **before** checking pool sufficiency. When `ErrInsufficientBonusPool` is returned, `slashRedelegationPosition` silently continues and calls `setPositionWithState` with the already-mutated `pos`, persisting the advanced checkpoints. The bonus for the pre-slash bonded segment is permanently unrecoverable.

---

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — Checkpoint mutation before pool check in `processEventsAndClaimBonus`:**

Inside the event loop, `pos.UpdateLastEventSeq(entry.Seq)` is called at line 193 and `k.decrementEventRefCount` is called at line 196 for every event. Both happen before the pool sufficiency check. [1](#0-0) 

After the loop, `applyBonusAccrualCheckpoint` and `pos.UpdateLastKnownBonded` are called at lines 215–217, also before the pool check. [2](#0-1) 

The pool check at line 230 is the **last** guard before payment: [3](#0-2) 

When the pool is empty, `ErrInsufficientBonusPool` is returned with `pos` already fully mutated in memory and event ref counts already decremented in the store.

**Step 2 — Silent skip in `slashRedelegationPosition`:**

The caller receives the error, logs it, and falls through unconditionally: [4](#0-3) 

**Step 3 — Mutated checkpoints are persisted:**

`setPositionWithState` is then called with the already-mutated `pos` (both partial-slash and full-slash branches): [5](#0-4) 

`setPositionWithState` unconditionally writes `pos` to the `Positions` collection: [6](#0-5) 

**Step 4 — Future `ClaimTierRewards` finds nothing:**

`processEventsAndClaimBonus` queries events with `seq > pos.LastEventSeq`: [7](#0-6) 

Since `LastEventSeq` was already advanced to the latest event, `getValidatorEventsSince` returns an empty slice. The bonus computation for the pre-slash bonded segment is skipped entirely. Additionally, `decrementEventRefCount` may have already garbage-collected those events from the store: [8](#0-7) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A position owner whose redelegation is slashed while `RewardsPoolName` has insufficient balance permanently loses all bonus rewards accrued during the pre-slash bonded segment. The checkpoints (`LastEventSeq`, `LastBonusAccrual`) are advanced and persisted, and the validator events are reference-count-decremented (potentially deleted), making the loss irrecoverable even after the pool is refilled. This is a direct, quantifiable financial loss proportional to the position's delegated shares, the tier's `BonusApy`, and the duration of the bonded segment.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The bonus pool (`RewardsPoolName`) is a module account funded externally. It can legitimately be empty or near-empty during early deployment, between funding cycles, or after a burst of claims. A redelegation slash is triggered by the Cosmos SDK staking module's `BeforeRedelegationSlashed` hook — a normal chain event requiring no attacker action. The combination of an empty pool and a redelegation slash is a realistic operational condition, not a contrived edge case.

---

### Recommendation

Move all in-memory checkpoint mutations and store-side `decrementEventRefCount` calls to **after** the pool sufficiency check succeeds. Specifically, restructure `processEventsAndClaimBonus` so that:

1. The event loop computes `totalBonus` and collects the events to process, but does **not** call `pos.UpdateLastEventSeq`, `applyBonusAccrualCheckpoint`, `pos.UpdateLastKnownBonded`, or `decrementEventRefCount` until after `sufficientBonusPoolBalance` passes and `SendCoinsFromModuleToAccount` succeeds.
2. Alternatively, if the pool-empty skip is intentional for chain-halt prevention, the caller (`slashRedelegationPosition`) must **not** persist the mutated `pos` when `ErrInsufficientBonusPool` is returned — it should re-fetch a fresh `pos` or explicitly reset the checkpoints to their pre-call values before calling `setPositionWithState`.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
1. Fund RewardsPoolName with a small amount (e.g., 1uatom).
2. Create a tier position with a redelegation; let the bonded segment accrue bonus > 1uatom.
3. Drain RewardsPoolName to zero (e.g., via a legitimate ClaimTierRewards from another position).
4. Trigger BeforeRedelegationSlashed for the position's unbonding ID.
   → slashRedelegationPosition fires, processEventsAndClaimBonus returns ErrInsufficientBonusPool,
     error is logged and skipped, setPositionWithState persists advanced LastEventSeq/LastBonusAccrual.
5. Refill RewardsPoolName with sufficient funds.
6. Call ClaimTierRewards for the position.
   → processEventsAndClaimBonus finds no events (LastEventSeq already at latest),
     computes zero bonus from the already-advanced LastBonusAccrual.
7. Assert bonus received == 0 despite a non-zero bonded segment having existed before the slash.
```
