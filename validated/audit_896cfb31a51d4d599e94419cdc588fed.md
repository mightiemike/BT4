### Title
`minDepositRate` Applied Symmetrically to Both Deposit and Borrow Multipliers Creates Unbacked Depositor Claims, Breaking Pool Solvency — (`SpotEngineState.sol`)

---

### Summary

In `SpotEngineState._updateState()`, the `minDepositRate` subsidy is applied by multiplying **both** `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` and `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18` by the same `minDepositRateMultiplierX18`. Because `totalDeposits > totalBorrows` (free liquidity always exists), depositors are credited more interest than borrowers pay. The surplus — `minDepositRate × freeLiquidity` per update cycle — is created from nothing. Over time, the sum of all depositor claims exceeds the actual token balance held by the contract, causing the last depositors to withdraw to fail.

---

### Finding Description

`SpotEngineState._updateState()` first computes a `depositRateMultiplierX18` that is carefully derived to maintain the invariant `deposits − borrows = constant` (free liquidity unchanged):

```
depositRateMultiplier = 1 + utilization × (borrowRateMultiplier − 1) × (1 − fee)
```

This is correct: the comment at line 102–113 explicitly documents this invariant. [1](#0-0) 

After that invariant-preserving step, the code applies `minDepositRate` to **both** cumulative multipliers:

```solidity
state.cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18 = state
    .cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18
    .mul(minDepositRateMultiplierX18);

state.cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18 = state
    .cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18
    .mul(minDepositRateMultiplierX18);
``` [2](#0-1) 

After this second multiplication the new free liquidity is:

```
newFreeLiquidity = (totalDeposits − totalBorrows) × minDepositRateMultiplier
                = freeLiquidity × minDepositRateMultiplier
```

The free liquidity tracked by the contract grows by `freeLiquidity × (minDepositRateMultiplier − 1)` every update. No tokens are transferred into the contract to back this growth. The actual ERC-20 balance of the clearinghouse is unchanged.

The `minDepositRateMultiplierX18` is itself a compound-interest multiplier:

```solidity
int128 minDepositRateMultiplierX18 = (ONE + minDepositRatePerSecondX18).pow(int128(dt));
``` [3](#0-2) 

`pow` is a true integer exponentiation in `MathSD21x18`: [4](#0-3) 

So the unbacked surplus compounds over time.

The existing `assertUtilization` guard only checks `totalDeposits >= totalBorrows`: [5](#0-4) 

This check is always satisfied because both sides are inflated by the same multiplier. It does not detect that the tracked free liquidity exceeds the real token balance.

---

### Impact Explanation

Every `updateStates` call with `minDepositRateX18 > 0` inflates depositor claims beyond what the contract holds. The deficit accumulates as:

```
deficit ≈ minDepositRate × freeLiquidity × time
```

When depositors attempt to withdraw, the contract pays out in FIFO order. The last depositors to withdraw will receive a revert because the ERC-20 transfer will fail — the contract no longer holds enough tokens to cover all outstanding claims. This is a direct solvency failure: the protocol becomes insolvent for a subset of depositors proportional to the accumulated deficit.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

`minDepositRateX18` is a first-class field in the `Config` struct and is explicitly checked and applied in `_updateState`. Any product configured with a non-zero `minDepositRateX18` triggers the issue on every `updateStates` call. `updateStates` is called by the sequencer via `Endpoint` on a regular cadence. No special attacker action is required — normal depositor/withdrawer activity is sufficient to expose the insolvency once the deficit is large enough. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Recommendation

The `minDepositRate` subsidy should only be applied to `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18`. The additional deposit interest it creates must be funded by a corresponding debit — either charged to borrowers (by also increasing `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18` only by the utilization-weighted portion) or drawn from the protocol insurance fund. Applying it symmetrically to both multipliers violates the invariant that `deposits − borrows` equals the real token balance.

A correct approach mirrors the base-interest derivation: compute the extra deposit-side cost, collect it from borrowers (or insurance), and only then credit depositors.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy a spot product with `minDepositRateX18 = 5e16` (5 % annualized).
2. Alice deposits 1 000 USDC. Bob borrows 500 USDC. Free liquidity = 500 USDC.
3. After one year of periodic `updateStates` calls:
   - `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` grows by `≈ 1.05^1` from `minDepositRate` alone.
   - Alice's tracked balance ≈ 1 050 USDC.
   - Bob owes ≈ 525 USDC (500 × 1.05).
   - Tracked free liquidity ≈ 525 USDC, but actual token balance = 500 USDC (unchanged).
   - Deficit ≈ 25 USDC (`minDepositRate × freeLiquidity × 1 year`).
4. Bob repays 525 USDC. Contract now holds 1 025 USDC.
5. Alice tries to withdraw 1 050 USDC → ERC-20 transfer reverts (contract only has 1 025 USDC).

The `assertUtilization` check passes throughout because `totalDeposits ≥ totalBorrows` is always true; it never detects the real-balance shortfall.
