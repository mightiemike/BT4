### Title
Sanctions Check Bypassed via Intermediary Contract in `claim()` — (`core/contracts/Airdrop.sol`)

### Summary
`_verifyProof` checks `isSanctioned(msg.sender)` but `msg.sender` is the calling contract address, not the EOA that controls it. A sanctioned EOA whose controlled contract holds a Merkle leaf can call `claim()` through that contract, pass the sanctions check, receive tokens, and forward them to the sanctioned EOA.

### Finding Description
`_verifyProof` enforces the sanctions invariant at line 54 using `sender = msg.sender`. [4](#0-3) 

When an intermediary smart contract is the direct caller, `msg.sender` is the contract address. If only the controlling EOA is sanctioned (not the contract itself), `isSanctioned` returns `false` and the check passes. Tokens are then sent to the intermediary (`msg.sender`) at line 71, which can immediately forward them to the sanctioned EOA. [5](#0-4) 

There is no `tx.origin` check, no check that `msg.sender` is an EOA, and no check that the ultimate recipient is not sanctioned.

### Impact Explanation
A sanctioned principal receives airdrop tokens in violation of the protocol's sanctions enforcement invariant. The asset delta is the full allocated amount for the intermediary's Merkle leaf. This is a direct, concrete asset transfer to a sanctioned party.

### Likelihood Explanation
Requires: (1) the intermediary contract has its own Merkle leaf (i.e., it was a genuine protocol participant before the snapshot), and (2) the controlling EOA is sanctioned after tree publication but before the claim is made. Both conditions are realistic in production — smart wallets, vaults, and multisigs regularly participate in protocols and their controllers can be sanctioned post-snapshot.

### Recommendation
Replace the `sender` check with a check against `tx.origin` as well, or add an explicit EOA-only guard (`require(msg.sender == tx.origin, "no contracts")`). Alternatively, check `isSanctioned` on both `msg.sender` and `tx.origin`:

```solidity
require(
    !ISanctionsList(sanctions).isSanctioned(msg.sender) &&
    !ISanctionsList(sanctions).isSanctioned(tx.origin),
    "address is sanctioned."
);
```
