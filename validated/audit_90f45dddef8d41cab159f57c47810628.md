### Title
`burnFee` Is Silently Destroyed and Never Credited to Any Account in `burnNlp()` - (File: `core/contracts/Clearinghouse.sol`)

---

### Summary

In `Clearinghouse.burnNlp()`, a `burnFee` is computed and subtracted from the user's returned `quoteAmount`, but it is **never credited to any protocol account** (`FEES_ACCOUNT`, `X_ACCOUNT`, or otherwise). The fee simply vanishes from the internal accounting system on every `burnNlp` call, permanently reducing the total tracked quote balance in the protocol.

---

### Finding Description

In `burnNlp()`, the fee is computed and applied as follows:

```solidity
int128 burnFee = MathHelper.max(ONE, quoteAmount / 1000);
quoteAmount = MathHelper.max(0, quoteAmount - burnFee);
```

The user's NLP is burned and they receive the reduced `quoteAmount`. The NLP pool receives `nlpAmount` back. But `burnFee` is never routed anywhere:

- It is not credited to `FEES_ACCOUNT` (as interest fees are in `SpotEngineState.sol`)
- It is not credited to `X_ACCOUNT` (as trading fees are in `OffchainExchange.dumpFees()`)
- It is not added to `marketInfo[productId].collectedFees`
- It is not added to `fees[productId]` in `BaseWithdrawPool`

The `burnFee` is simply dropped. Every call to `burnNlp` permanently destroys at least `ONE` (1e18) units of internal quote balance from the system's accounting, creating a growing divergence between the protocol's internal balance sheet and actual ERC20 holdings.

This is structurally identical to the ZetaChain M-16 bug: a fee amount is included in a computed total, the non-fee portion is correctly routed, but the fee portion is left with no destination and accumulates as a permanent loss.

---

### Impact Explanation

Each `burnNlp` call leaks `burnFee = max(1e18, quoteAmount / 1000)` from the protocol's internal accounting. Over time, the aggregate internal QUOTE balance tracked across all subaccounts becomes less than the actual ERC20 token balance held by the contract. This is a solvency accounting corruption: the protocol's internal ledger understates total liabilities relative to assets, meaning the "missing" quote is effectively destroyed — it cannot be claimed by any party, including the protocol itself. At scale, this represents a permanent, irreversible loss of protocol revenue.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

`burnNlp` is a standard user-facing operation reachable by any holder of NLP tokens through the `Endpoint`. No special privileges are required. The fee is non-zero on every call (`max(ONE, quoteAmount / 1000)`), so the accounting leak occurs unconditionally on every execution.

---

### Recommendation

Credit `burnFee` to a designated protocol account immediately after computing it. The pattern used elsewhere in the codebase for interest fees is:

```solidity
spotEngine.updateBalance(QUOTE_PRODUCT_ID, FEES_ACCOUNT, burnFee);
```

Alternatively, credit it to `X_ACCOUNT` consistent with how `dumpFees()` routes collected trading fees.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. User holds NLP tokens and calls `burnNlp` via `Endpoint.submitSlowModeTx`.
2. `Clearinghouse.burnNlp()` executes:
   - `quoteAmount = nlpAmount * oraclePriceX18` (e.g., 1000e18)
   - `burnFee = max(1e18, 1000e18 / 1000) = 1e18`
   - `quoteAmount = 999e18`
   - `spotEngine.updateBalance(QUOTE_PRODUCT_ID, txn.sender, 999e18)` — user credited
   - `spotEngine.updateBalance(NLP_PRODUCT_ID, N_ACCOUNT, nlpAmount)` — pool credited
   - `burnFee` (1e18) is never credited anywhere
3. The total internal QUOTE balance in the system is now 1e18 less than before the call, with no corresponding reduction in ERC20 holdings.
4. Repeated calls accumulate this deficit permanently.
