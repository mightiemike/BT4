### Title
Global `lastLiquidationFees` Contaminated by Cross-Subaccount Liquidations Causes Over-Socialization of Depositor Losses — (`core/contracts/ClearinghouseLiq.sol`)

---

### Summary

`lastLiquidationFees` is a single global `int128` state variable in `ClearinghouseStorage`. It is written by every call to `_handleLiquidationPayment` regardless of which subaccount is being liquidated, and read by `_finalizeSubaccount` to reserve fees from insurance before socialization. Because the variable is not scoped per-subaccount, liquidating any subaccount between another subaccount's last liquidation step and its finalization step overwrites the value, causing `_finalizeSubaccount` to use the wrong fee amount. When the stale value is larger than the correct one, less insurance is presented to `perpEngine.socializeSubaccount`, causing more depositor losses to be socialized than necessary.

---

### Finding Description

**Storage declaration** — `lastLiquidationFees` is a single contract-level slot: [1](#0-0) 

**Write site** — every call to `_handleLiquidationPayment` unconditionally overwrites it with the fees from whatever subaccount was just liquidated: [2](#0-1) 

The comment at lines 581–585 explains the design intent: reserve the fees just added to insurance so they are not immediately consumed during socialization, preventing a blocking condition. The assumption is that `lastLiquidationFees` always reflects the fees from the immediately preceding liquidation step **of the same subaccount**.

**Read sites** — `_finalizeSubaccount` subtracts the value before socialization and adds it back after: [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3) 

**The contamination path:**

```
tx_prev: liquidateSubaccountImpl(subaccountB, productId=X)
         → _handleLiquidationPayment → lastLiquidationFees = feesB

tx1:     liquidateSubaccountImpl(subaccountA, productId=Y)
         → _handleLiquidationPayment → lastLiquidationFees = feesA  ← overwrites feesB

tx2:     liquidateSubaccountImpl(subaccountB, productId=uint32.max)
         → _finalizeSubaccount reads lastLiquidationFees = feesA    ← stale
```

`liquidateSubaccountImpl` is `external` and each call is an independent sequencer transaction: [5](#0-4) 

There is no atomicity guarantee between subaccountB's last liquidation step and its finalization. Any liquidator can submit a liquidation for subaccountA in between.

---

### Impact Explanation

When `feesA > feesB` (the stale value is larger than the correct one):

- Line 369 computes `v.insurance = insurance - feesA`, presenting **less** insurance to `perpEngine.socializeSubaccount` than the correct `insurance - feesB`.
- `perpEngine.socializeSubaccount(txn.liquidatee, v.insurance)` distributes more of subaccountB's losses to depositors because less insurance is available to absorb them.
- Line 410 adds back `feesA` (not `feesB`), so the stored `insurance` is `socialize(insurance - feesA) - insuranceCover + feesA`.
- The depositors have permanently absorbed `feesA - feesB` more in losses than they should have, while the insurance fund retains that excess — a direct transfer of losses from the insurance fund to depositors.

This satisfies the "bad debt creation" criterion: depositors are forced to absorb losses that the insurance fund had sufficient capital to cover.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- Requires no special privileges. Any liquidator can liquidate subaccountA between subaccountB's steps.
- In a busy market with many simultaneous liquidations (exactly the scenario where the insurance fund is under stress and socialization is most likely to trigger), interleaving is the norm, not the exception.
- The attacker does not need to control the sequencer — they only need to submit a valid liquidation transaction for any subaccount with large fees before subaccountB is finalized.

---

### Recommendation

Replace the single global `lastLiquidationFees` with a per-subaccount mapping:

```solidity
// ClearinghouseStorage.sol
mapping(bytes32 => int128) internal lastLiquidationFees;
```

Update `_handleLiquidationPayment` to write `lastLiquidationFees[txn.liquidatee] = v.liquidationFees` and `_finalizeSubaccount` to read `lastLiquidationFees[txn.liquidatee]`, clearing it after use. This ensures the reserved fee amount always corresponds to the subaccount being finalized.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
State: insurance = 1000, feesB = 10, feesA = 500
subaccountB has perp losses = 600 (needs socialization)

Correct execution (no interleaving):
  lastLiquidationFees = 10 (feesB)
  v.insurance = 1000 - 10 = 990
  perpEngine.socializeSubaccount(B, 990) → uses 600, returns 390
  insurance stored = 390 + 10 = 400
  Depositors socialized: 0 (insurance covered all losses)

Stale execution (feesA=500 overwrites feesB=10):
  lastLiquidationFees = 500 (feesA, stale)
  v.insurance = 1000 - 500 = 500
  perpEngine.socializeSubaccount(B, 500) → uses 500, returns 0; remaining 100 socialized to depositors
  insurance stored = 0 + 500 = 500
  Depositors socialized: 100 (should have been 0)
  Insurance over-retained: 500 (should have been 400)
```

Depositors absorb 100 in losses that the insurance fund had sufficient capital to cover. The insurance fund retains 100 more than it should. The invariant `lastLiquidationFees used in _finalizeSubaccount == fees from the immediately preceding step of the same subaccount's liquidation sequence` is broken.
