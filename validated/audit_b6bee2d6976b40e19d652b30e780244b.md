Based on my analysis of the repository, I can identify a valid analog to the OracleMaker premium calculation vulnerability.

---

### Title
`topUpBaseRewards` Uses User-Inflatable `totalBonded` to Compute Shortfall, Draining the Shared `RewardsPool` and Starving Tiered-Position Bonus Rewards — (File: `x/tieredrewards/keeper/abci.go`)

---

### Summary

`topUpBaseRewards` computes the per-block base-reward shortfall as `totalBonded × targetBaseRewardsRate / blocksPerYear`. Because `totalBonded` is directly inflatable by any delegator through a standard `MsgDelegate` transaction, an attacker can temporarily inflate the shortfall, causing the shared `RewardsPool` to be drained at a rate far above what the protocol intended. The same pool funds bonus rewards for tiered-position holders; draining it deprives them of their promised bonus APY.

---

### Finding Description

In `topUpBaseRewards`, the target per-block reward is computed directly from the live `totalBonded` value: [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

```go
totalBonded, err := k.stakingKeeper.TotalBondedTokens(ctx)
// ...
targetStakersRewardPerBlock := math.LegacyNewDecFromInt(totalBonded).
    Mul(targetBaseRewardsRate).
    Quo(math.LegacyNewDec(int64(blocksPerYear)))
```

The shortfall is then: [3](#0-2) 

```go
defaultStakersRewardPerBlock := math.LegacyNewDecFromInt(feeCollectorBalance.Amount).
    MulTruncate(math.LegacyOneDec().Sub(communityTax))
shortFallAmount := targetStakersRewardPerBlock.Sub(defaultStakersRewardPerBlock).TruncateInt()
```

Any positive shortfall is immediately transferred out of the `RewardsPool`: [4](#0-3) 

```go
err = k.bankKeeper.SendCoinsFromModuleToModule(ctx, types.RewardsPoolName, distributiontypes.ModuleName, ...)
```

`totalBonded` is the aggregate of all bonded delegations across every validator. Any account can increase it by submitting a `MsgDelegate`. There is no snapshot, time-lock, or epoch boundary — the value read at line 36 is the live state at the start of each block.

The `RewardsPool` (`types.RewardsPoolName`) is the **same** module account used to pay out bonus rewards to tiered-position holders. When `topUpBaseRewards` drains it, there is less (or nothing) left for bonus-reward claims.

**Structural parallel to the OracleMaker bug:**

| OracleMaker | `topUpBaseRewards` |
|---|---|
| `maxPositionNotional = collateralForOpen / minMarginRatio` | `targetStakersRewardPerBlock = totalBonded × rate / blocksPerYear` |
| User deposits collateral → denominator grows → premium shrinks | User delegates tokens → numerator grows → shortfall grows |
| User opens position at reduced premium, then withdraws | User earns base rewards funded from `RewardsPool`, then undelegates |
| Free collateral should not influence the premium rate | Live `totalBonded` should not be the sole driver of per-block drain |

---

### Impact Explanation

The `RewardsPool` is finite. Suppose fee revenue already covers the existing stakers (shortfall = 0 without the attacker). After the attacker delegates `X` tokens:

- `targetStakersRewardPerBlock` rises by `X × rate / blocksPerYear`
- `shortFallAmount` becomes `X × rate / blocksPerYear`
- The `RewardsPool` is drained by that amount **every block** for as long as the delegation is active

The attacker receives their proportional share of the newly created shortfall as base staking rewards. The remainder of the drain is distributed to other validators, but the net effect is that the `RewardsPool` — which is the sole funding source for tiered-position bonus rewards — is depleted at an attacker-controlled rate. Tiered-position holders who locked tokens for a fixed duration in exchange for a guaranteed bonus APY receive zero (or reduced) bonus rewards once the pool is empty. [5](#0-4) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The entry path is a standard, unprivileged `MsgDelegate` transaction — no special role or permission is required. The 21-day Cosmos SDK unbonding period means the attacker cannot exit atomically, but:

- The drain occurs every block during the delegation period, so even a single large delegation causes sustained damage.
- The attacker recovers their principal after unbonding; their cost is only the opportunity cost of locked tokens.
- A well-capitalised actor (e.g., an exchange or whale) can execute this with existing holdings.

The OracleMaker report was accepted at medium severity despite a similar "requires a delay" constraint.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Decouple the shortfall denominator from live `totalBonded`.** Use a snapshotted or epoch-averaged `totalBonded` (e.g., captured at the start of each reward epoch) so that intra-epoch delegations do not immediately inflate the shortfall.

2. **Separate the `RewardsPool` into two sub-accounts** — one for base-reward top-up and one for bonus rewards — so that `topUpBaseRewards` cannot deplete the bonus-reward reserve.

3. **Cap the per-block drain** from `topUpBaseRewards` to a governance-controlled maximum, independent of `totalBonded`.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Observe current `totalBonded = Y` and `feeCollectorBalance = D` such that `D × (1 − communityTax) ≥ Y × rate / blocksPerYear` (no shortfall today).
2. Attacker submits `MsgDelegate` for `X ≫ Y` tokens to any bonded validator.
3. Next `BeginBlocker` call: `totalBonded = Y + X`; `targetStakersRewardPerBlock = (Y+X) × rate / blocksPerYear`; `shortFallAmount ≈ X × rate / blocksPerYear`.
4. `topUpBaseRewards` transfers `shortFallAmount` from `RewardsPool` → `distribution` module every block.
5. Attacker's validator receives `X/(Y+X)` of the shortfall as staking rewards each block; attacker collects them.
6. After sufficient blocks the `RewardsPool` is empty; tiered-position holders' `ClaimRewards` calls find no bonus funds.
7. Attacker submits `MsgUndelegate`; after the 21-day unbonding period, principal is returned intact. [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6)
