### Title
Funding Rate Cap Computed Using Spot Oracle Price at Update Time Instead of Average Index Price Over the Period — (File: `core/contracts/PerpEngineState.sol`)

---

### Summary
`updateStates()` in `PerpEngineState` caps the incoming `avgPriceDiffs` using the **current oracle price at the moment of the call** rather than the average index price over the elapsed period `dt`. This is a direct structural analog to M-03: a time-accumulated value is bounded by a spot price that may have moved significantly during the accumulation window, causing the funding cap to be either inflated or deflated relative to what the protocol intends.

---

### Finding Description

In `PerpEngineState.updateStates()`, the sequencer supplies `avgPriceDiffs[i]` — the average mark-minus-index price difference over the elapsed period `dt`. The contract then caps this value before computing the funding payment:

```solidity
// core/contracts/PerpEngineState.sol  lines 116–130
int128 indexPriceX18 = _risk(productId).priceX18;   // ← spot price NOW

int128 priceDiffX18 = avgPriceDiffs[i];

int128 maxPriceDiff = MAX_DAILY_FUNDING_RATE.mul(indexPriceX18); // ← cap based on spot price NOW

if (priceDiffX18.abs() > maxPriceDiff) {
    priceDiffX18 = (priceDiffX18 > 0) ? maxPriceDiff : -maxPriceDiff;
}

int128 paymentAmount = priceDiffX18.mul(dtX18).div(ONE_DAY_X18);
state.cumulativeFundingLongX18 += paymentAmount;
state.cumulativeFundingShortX18 += paymentAmount;
```

`_risk(productId).priceX18` is the oracle price that was last written by `updatePrice`, which reflects the **current** market price at the time `updateStates` is called — not the average index price over the `dt` window. The cap `MAX_DAILY_FUNDING_RATE` (2%) is intended to bound the funding rate as a fraction of the index price, but the index price used is the spot price at the end of the period, not the average over the period.

The `dt` window can be up to `7 * SECONDS_PER_DAY` (line 114), meaning the oracle price at call time can differ substantially from the average index price over the window. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

The call path is: `EndpointTx` processes a `UpdateFundingRate`-type transaction and calls `perpEngine.updateStates(dt, txn.avgPriceDiffs)` at line 483. [3](#0-2) 

---

### Impact Explanation

The corrupted state is `state.cumulativeFundingLongX18` and `state.cumulativeFundingShortX18` stored in `PerpEngineState.states[productId]`. [4](#0-3) 

These accumulators are consumed in `_updateBalance` to compute `diffX18.mul(balance.amount)`, which is subtracted from every position's `vQuoteBalance` on the next balance interaction. [5](#0-4) 

Two concrete scenarios:

**Scenario A — Price spike at end of period**: The oracle price doubles just before `updateStates` is called. `maxPriceDiff` is now twice as large as it should be relative to the average index price over the period. A `priceDiffX18` that should have been capped passes through uncapped (or is capped at a higher value), inflating `paymentAmount`. Longs pay (or shorts receive) more funding than the 2% daily cap was designed to allow, constituting an accounting corruption that transfers wealth from one side of the book to the other.

**Scenario B — Price drop at end of period**: The oracle price halves just before `updateStates` is called. `maxPriceDiff` is now half of what it should be. A legitimate `avgPriceDiffs[i]` that is within the intended 2% cap relative to the average price gets incorrectly truncated, underpaying funding to the deserving side.

In both cases, `vQuoteBalance` for all open perp positions is permanently miscalculated, affecting PnL, health checks, and settlement amounts. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Medium. The `dt` window is bounded only by `dt < 7 * SECONDS_PER_DAY`, meaning a single `updateStates` call can cover up to a week of funding accumulation. In volatile markets, a 20–50%+ price move over a multi-day window is common. The oracle price is updated independently via `updatePrice` transactions, so the price at the time of `updateStates` routinely differs from the average price over `dt`. No attacker action is required — this occurs in normal protocol operation whenever price moves during the funding window. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Recommendation

Pass the average index price over the `dt` period as an additional parameter to `updateStates` (analogous to how `avgPriceDiffs` is already the average mark-index diff, not the spot diff). The cap should be:

```solidity
int128 maxPriceDiff = MAX_DAILY_FUNDING_RATE.mul(avgIndexPriceX18);
```

where `avgIndexPriceX18` is computed off-chain over the same window as `avgPriceDiffs` and supplied alongside it. This mirrors the recommendation in M-03 to use the average price over the staking period rather than the spot price at claim time.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. At `t=0`, BTC perp oracle price = 50,000 USD. A user holds a long position of 1 BTC.
2. Over the next 24 hours (`dt = 86400`), the average mark-index diff is 900 USD (1.8% of 50,000 — within the 2% cap at average price).
3. Just before `updateStates` is called at `t=86400`, the oracle price is updated to 100,000 USD (price doubled).
4. On-chain: `maxPriceDiff = 0.02 * 100,000 = 2,000 USD`. The 900 USD diff passes uncapped.
5. `paymentAmount = 900 * 86400 / 86400 = 900` per unit of position.
6. Had the cap been applied at the average price (50,000): `maxPriceDiff = 1,000 USD` — still passes, but in a scenario where `avgPriceDiffs[i] = 1,500 USD` (3% of average price, should be capped at 2%):
   - Correct behavior: cap to `0.02 * 50,000 = 1,000`, `paymentAmount = 1,000`.
   - Actual behavior: cap to `0.02 * 100,000 = 2,000`, `paymentAmount = 1,500` — 50% more funding extracted from longs than the protocol cap permits.

The corrupted `cumulativeFundingLongX18` persists in storage and affects every subsequent `_updateBalance` call for all long position holders on that product. [8](#0-7)
