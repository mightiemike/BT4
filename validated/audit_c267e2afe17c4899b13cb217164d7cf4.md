### Title
Premature Socialization via Global `lastLiquidationFees` Deduction in `_finalizeSubaccount` — (`core/contracts/ClearinghouseLiq.sol`)

---

### Summary

`_finalizeSubaccount` unconditionally deducts the global `lastLiquidationFees` from the effective insurance before deciding whether to socialize losses. Because `lastLiquidationFees` is a single global slot (not per-liquidatee), a liquidator who sequences a regular liquidation immediately before a finalization call can cause the protocol to trigger `spotEngine.socializeSubaccount` on a subaccount whose liabilities the insurance fund was fully capable of covering — spreading bad debt to innocent users.

---

### Finding Description

**Storage layout**

`lastLiquidationFees` is declared as a single global `int128` in `ClearinghouseStorage`: [1](#0-0) 

It is overwritten on every call to `_handleLiquidationPayment`, regardless of which subaccount is being liquidated: [2](#0-1) 

**Finalization deduction**

Inside `_finalizeSubaccount`, the effective insurance used for all socialization decisions is computed as:

```
v.insurance = insurance;
v.insurance -= lastLiquidationFees;          // line 369
v.canLiquidateMore = (quoteBalance.amount + v.insurance) > 0;
``` [3](#0-2) 

The fees are added back only *after* the socialization decision has already been executed: [4](#0-3) 

**The broken invariant**

Let:
- `I` = `insurance` at the moment finalization is called (includes fees just added by the preceding regular liquidation)
- `F` = `lastLiquidationFees` (fees from the most recent regular liquidation of *any* subaccount)
- `L` = absolute value of the liquidatee's remaining USDC deficit

The protocol socializes when `v.insurance = I - F < L`, i.e. when `I < L + F`.

But the actual insurance available is `I`. If `I >= L`, the fund is solvent and socialization must not occur. The deduction creates a window `L ≤ I < L + F` where socialization fires despite the fund being sufficient.

**Exploit sequence**

The entry point is the signed `LiquidateSubaccount` transaction processed by the endpoint: [5](#0-4) 

which calls `Clearinghouse.liquidateSubaccount` (guarded `onlyEndpoint`) via `delegatecall` into `ClearinghouseLiq.liquidateSubaccountImpl`: [6](#0-5) 

A liquidator submits two consecutive signed transactions to the sequencer:

1. **Regular liquidation** of subaccount X (or any other under-maintenance subaccount A) — sets `lastLiquidationFees = F`, adds `F` to `insurance`.
2. **Finalization** (`productId == type(uint32).max`) of the target subaccount B — `_finalizeSubaccount` deducts `F` from `v.insurance`, making it appear the fund cannot cover `L_B`, and calls `spotEngine.socializeSubaccount(txn.liquidatee)`.

Because `lastLiquidationFees` is global, step 1 can target a *different* subaccount (A) from the one being finalized (B), giving the liquidator full control over the magnitude of `F`.

---

### Impact Explanation

When `L_B ≤ insurance < L_B + F`:

- `v.insurance = insurance - F < L_B` → `insuranceCover` covers only part of the deficit.
- `v.insurance` reaches `≤ 0` → `spotEngine.socializeSubaccount(txn.liquidatee)` is called.
- The remaining deficit is spread across all users holding the socialized asset, creating bad debt for innocent counterparties.
- After socialization, `v.insurance += lastLiquidationFees` restores the fees to the insurance slot — confirming the fund was never actually insolvent.

This directly matches the Critical scope: **premature socialization of losses to other subaccounts when insurance was actually sufficient, creating bad debt for innocent users**.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- The liquidator is an externally reachable actor submitting signed transactions through the normal liquidation path — no admin access or key compromise required.
- In volatile markets, insurance hovering just above a liquidatee's deficit is a common condition.
- The liquidator can tune `F` by choosing the size of the preceding regular liquidation (subject only to the constraint that the liquidated subaccount is under maintenance and the amount passes `_assertLiquidationAmount`).
- The two transactions can be submitted back-to-back in the same sequencer batch, making the window deterministic.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Make `lastLiquidationFees` per-liquidatee** rather than a global slot, so the deduction in `_finalizeSubaccount` only reflects fees earned from liquidating *that specific subaccount*.
2. **Guard the deduction with a solvency check**: only deduct `lastLiquidationFees` when `insurance - lastLiquidationFees < L`; if `insurance >= L`, skip the deduction entirely and cover the full deficit without triggering socialization.
3. Alternatively, remove the deduction altogether and instead track whether the subaccount was already determined to be insolvent before the last fee accrual, using a per-subaccount flag.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
Setup:
  insurance = 105
  lastLiquidationFees = 0
  SubaccountB.quoteBalance = -100   (all other positions = 0, ready to finalize)

Step 1 — Regular liquidation of SubaccountA:
  _handleLiquidationPayment generates liquidationFees = 10
  insurance += 10  →  insurance = 115
  lastLiquidationFees = 10

Step 2 — Finalization of SubaccountB (productId = type(uint32).max):
  v.insurance = 115
  v.insurance -= lastLiquidationFees (10)  →  v.insurance = 105
  quoteBalance.amount = -100
  insuranceCover = min(105, 100) = 100
  v.insurance -= 100  →  v.insurance = 5
  // v.insurance > 0, so no socialization here

  Wait — let me use the tighter window:

Setup (revised):
  insurance = 108   (after step 1 added F=10, so pre-step-1 insurance was 98)
  lastLiquidationFees = 10
  SubaccountB.quoteBalance = -100

  v.insurance = 108 - 10 = 98
  insuranceCover = min(98, 100) = 98
  v.insurance = 98 - 98 = 0
  SubaccountB.quoteBalance = -100 + 98 = -2  (still negative)
  v.insurance <= 0  →  spotEngine.socializeSubaccount(SubaccountB)  ← TRIGGERED
  v.insurance += 10  →  v.insurance = 10
  insurance = 10

Assert: socialization fired even though insurance (108) > liabilities (100).
Without the deduction: insuranceCover = min(108,100) = 100, v.insurance = 8 > 0, no socialization.
``` [7](#0-6)
